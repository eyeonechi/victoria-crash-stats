We see a pretty even distribution, with Fridays and Saturdays having slightly higher averages.

More people tend to go out during the weekend.

Collisions with vehicles seem the most common of the accident types.

More people are injured and killed when colliding with fixed objects.

Trees and concrete structures can prove to be fatal to speeding vehicles.

Contrary to beliefs, most accidents happen during the daytime.

This might be so since most people prefer to drive during the day.

It is also possible that people exercise more caution during the night.

Complicated road structures like intersections have higher average incident rates

However, most of the incidents involve drivers away from intersections.

Bad road conditions of non-city roads may be a factor causing this.

There is an increasing trend as more people are involved at higher speed zones.

Driving faster causes more likelyhood to be involved in an accident.

A total of 142573 accidents have been recorded from 2006 till 2016.

From the map, most accidents are concentrated around the metropolitan areas.

This could be due to the higher volume of traffic present in Melbourne and its surrounding suburbs.
